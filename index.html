Rails Bridge DC

RailsBridge workshops are a free and fun way to get started or level up with Rails, Ruby, and other web technologies.

Our events focus on increasing diversity in tech, so that people of all backgrounds can feel welcome and comfortable in our industry.

As an organization whose output is entirely open source, all of RailsBridge's growth is a result of the efforts of members of the community — people who see an opportunity for improvement and make it happen.

We're looking for volunteer instructors and assistants to facilitate Ruby/HTML/CSS training sessions. Have a few hours to share with eager women (and friends) learning to code?

APPLY

Learn

Our most well-known efforts are free weekend workshops where women learn Ruby on Rails from volunteers. The students at our workshops range from folks who are completely new to programming to QA engineers and professional developers who want to learn Rails.

During the Installfest (usually on a Friday evening), we get students' laptops setup with the requisite technologies. The next day, we break into small group based on experience level and build a web app! (When we aren't teaching Ruby and Rails, we teach HTML and CSS.)

JOIN US

Do

We teach people to code because we believe that the people making technology should accurately reflect the diversity of those using it. We want to push for all kinds of diversity in tech: gender, race, sexual orientation, ability, and class. Women were the first population we focused on, but aren't the last.

Do you share our values? We're looking for assistant organizers, community managers, sponsors, and donors to help us share Ruby with our community. Share a few hours a month and watch inspired minds create.
